Reviewing Criteria

RIDE reviews are based on transparent, community-developed criteria published by the Institute for Documentology and Scholarly Editing (IDE). Each review category has its own catalogue of criteria:

Scholarly Editions (SE)

Reviews of digital scholarly editions follow the Criteria for Reviewing Scholarly Digital Editions (Version 1.1). The criteria cover aspects such as:

  • Scholarly quality and critical engagement with the source
  • Technical implementation and sustainability
  • Documentation and transparency
  • Usability and design

Digital Text Collections (DTC)

Reviews of digital text collections follow the Criteria for Reviewing Digital Text Collections (Version 1.0). The criteria address:

  • Selection and scope of the collection
  • Quality of digitisation and encoding
  • Search and access functionality
  • Sustainability and documentation

Tools & Environments (TE)

Reviews of tools and environments follow the Criteria for Reviewing Tools and Environments for Digital Scholarly Editing (Version 1.0). The criteria evaluate:

  • Functionality and features
  • Usability and documentation
  • Interoperability and standards compliance
  • Sustainability and community support

Questionnaires

Each review is accompanied by a structured questionnaire (factsheet) that systematically evaluates the reviewed resource against the relevant criteria. The factsheets allow for quantitative comparison across reviews and are available as part of the RIDE dataset.

Writing and Submission Guidelines

Authors preparing reviews for RIDE should consult the Writing Guidelines and Submission Guidelines.
