RIDE reviews are based on transparent, community-developed criteria published by
the Institute for Documentology and Scholarly Editing (IDE). Each review category
has its own catalogue of criteria:

## Scholarly Editions (SE)

Reviews of digital scholarly editions follow the
[Criteria for Reviewing Scholarly Digital Editions](https://www.i-d-e.de/publikationen/weitereschriften/criteria-version-1-1/)
(Version 1.1). The criteria cover aspects such as:

- Scholarly quality and critical engagement with the source
- Technical implementation and sustainability
- Documentation and transparency
- Usability and design

## Digital Text Collections (DTC)

Reviews of digital text collections follow the
[Criteria for Reviewing Digital Text Collections](https://www.i-d-e.de/publikationen/weitereschriften/criteria-text-collections-version-1-0/)
(Version 1.0). The criteria address:

- Selection and scope of the collection
- Quality of digitisation and encoding
- Search and access functionality
- Sustainability and documentation

## Tools & Environments (TE)

Reviews of tools and environments follow the
[Criteria for Reviewing Tools and Environments for Digital Scholarly Editing](https://www.i-d-e.de/publikationen/weitereschriften/criteria-tools-version-1-0/)
(Version 1.0). The criteria evaluate:

- Functionality and features
- Usability and documentation
- Interoperability and standards compliance
- Sustainability and community support

## Questionnaires

Each review is accompanied by a structured questionnaire (factsheet) that
systematically evaluates the reviewed resource against the relevant criteria.
The factsheets allow for quantitative comparison across reviews and are
available as part of the [RIDE dataset](https://github.com/i-d-e/ride).

## Writing and Submission Guidelines

Authors preparing reviews for RIDE should consult the
[Writing Guidelines](https://ride.i-d-e.de/reviewers/writing-guidelines/) and
[Submission Guidelines](https://ride.i-d-e.de/reviewers/submission-guidelines/).
